Jumper

What is a Jumper?

A jumper is a small electrical device used to connect two pins or terminals in an electronic circuit. It is typically used to close or open a circuit, making it easy to modify or repair the circuit without needing to solder or make permanent changes.

Types of Jumpers

Pin Jumper:

A pin jumper consists of a small piece of wire or metal that is used to connect two pins or terminals on a circuit board or device.

Uses: Connecting or disconnecting circuit components, configuring jumpers on circuit boards.

Wire Jumper:

Wire jumpers are flexible wires with connectors on both ends, used to create temporary or permanent electrical connections between points in a circuit.

Uses: Creating connections between circuit components, patching broken circuits.

Bridge Jumper:

Bridge jumpers are typically used to connect two distant points in a circuit where wiring is impractical. They can also be used to create short-circuits for testing purposes.

Uses: Testing circuits, connecting distant pins in a circuit board.

Applications of Jumpers

Jumpers are used in various applications, including:

  • Connecting circuit components in prototype boards
  • Configuring settings on circuit boards
  • Testing and troubleshooting electrical circuits
