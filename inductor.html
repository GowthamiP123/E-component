Inductor

What is an Inductor?

An inductor is a passive electrical component that stores energy in the form of a magnetic field. It consists of a coil of wire, usually wound around a core material such as iron or ferrite. When current flows through the coil, a magnetic field is generated. Inductors resist changes in current and are often used in circuits for filtering, energy storage, and inductive loads.

Types of Inductors

Air Core Inductor:

Air core inductors have no magnetic core and rely solely on the coil to generate a magnetic field. These are commonly used in high-frequency circuits.

Uses: High-frequency circuits, radio transmitters, and receivers.

Iron Core Inductor:

Iron core inductors use a magnetic core made of iron or steel to increase the inductance and improve efficiency. These are often used in power applications.

Uses: Power supplies, transformers, and inductive heating.

Ferrite Core Inductor:

Ferrite core inductors use ferrite material for the core, which is a type of ceramic material. Ferrite cores help to increase the inductance while keeping the size of the inductor small.

Uses: Power supplies, filters, and signal processing circuits.

Applications of Inductors

Inductors are used in many different applications, such as:

  • Energy storage in power supply circuits
  • Filters to block unwanted frequencies
  • Transformers for voltage conversion
  • Inductive loads in motors and relays

பயன்பாடுகள்: பவர் சப்ளைகள், வடிகட்டி, மற்றும் சிக்னல் செயலாக்க சுற்றுகள்.
