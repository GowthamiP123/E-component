Wire

Wires are the basic parts of electrical systems. They carry electricity from one place to another, making our lights, fans, and gadgets work. Wires come in different types, each made for a specific job.

Types of Wires

Simple Wire:

This type of wire is made of a single metal strand. It is strong and is usually used in walls for electrical connections at home.

Example: Wires behind your light switches.

Flexible Wire:

This wire is made of many thin strands of metal twisted together. It bends easily and is used for things that move a lot, like chargers and car batteries.

Example: The wire in your phone charger.

TV Cable:

This is a special wire with layers to protect it from outside interference. It is used to connect TVs and internet modems.

Example: The cable for your internet connection.

Network Wire:

This wire has two strands twisted together to reduce signal problems. It is used for internet and phone connections.

Example: The cable that connects your computer to the internet router.

Everyday Uses of Wires

In Homes:

Wires connect lights, fans, and switches in your house to the power supply.

In Cars:

Wires in cars connect the battery to lights, radios, and other parts that need power.

In Gadgets:

Wires inside gadgets like phones, laptops, and TVs make them work by transferring electricity and signals.
